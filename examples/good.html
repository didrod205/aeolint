How to Brew Pour-Over Coffee

Pour-over coffee is brewed by slowly pouring hot water over ground coffee in a paper filter. The short version: use a 1:16 coffee-to-water ratio, a medium-fine grind, water at 96°C, and aim for a total brew time of about three minutes.

What you need

  • A pour-over dripper and paper filters
  • A burr grinder
  • A gooseneck kettle and a scale

How much coffee should I use?

Use a 1:16 ratio as a starting point. For a single mug that's roughly 22 grams of coffee to 350 grams of water. Adjust to taste: more coffee for a stronger cup, less for a lighter one.

What grind size works best?

Aim for a medium-fine grind, similar to table salt. Too fine and the brew is bitter and slow; too coarse and it tastes weak and sour.

Frequently asked questions

How long should the brew take?

About three minutes total, including a 30-second bloom.

Does water temperature matter?

Yes — 92–96°C extracts evenly. Boiling water can scorch the grounds.
